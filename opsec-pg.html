• How can I become untrackeble?

There is no true anonymity, if someone powerful enough wants to find you, they will. but that doesn't mean you can't make that harder:

-Get a good, trusted vpn. Most vpn's on the market are not as secure as they apper, but it will atleast add a middle-man if someone what to see your traffic

-If you can, use proxy chains. A lot of people (hackers mostly) got tracked down because they ddint hide their ip, I recommend using proxy-chains which act very similar to a vpn but you can set as many as you want

-Don't do anything dumb like saying you name on the internet, you addres , or anything from your personal life, this is how most hackers or wanted people where found , we will learn more about this in "Bad OpSec"

-One of the most common suggestions in this domain is having a diffrent laptop for all you dangerous activity online, but I think that is kind of going overboard and a live usb** should be enough (**- a live usb means a thumb drive that has a operating sistem running on it so you need only plug the usb in the pc to boot it up) my favourite os is kali linux but parrot works very well as well

-One of my personal recommendations is having two separate emails for your personal life (social media apps) and your internet life (any kind of site you dont want to know anything about you).

• Bad Opsec

In this section, we will talk about, you guessed it, 'Bad Opsec' and the people who have been caught doing illegal activities. Dread Pirate Roberts / Ross Ulbricht:This is one of the most infamous cases of bad Opsec. This guy ran a huge darknet marketplace where you could buy and sell almost anything. But, of course, pride got the better of him, so he started talking about it on a forum where he forgot to change his old name. Consequently, the FBI tracked the server with the same name to him. Along with a post about hiring a web developer and a 'cryptocurrency expert,' it was enough to get him caught.
PomPomPurin:A little background on this guy he was an active member of a darknet forum where leaked databases were sold. After that respective site got taken down by the FBI, he made a new one. The biggest mistake he made was talking to the admin about a leaked database in which he should have been a part of but wasn't. To prove that the database was not complete, he gave a 'random' example of an email address he knew for sure should have been in the leak but was not. Unfortunately, that email address turned out to be his own, with his full name. Obviously, after the database was taken by the feds, they had full access to that conversation, and that's how they got the full name of the admin of one of the biggest darknet forums
